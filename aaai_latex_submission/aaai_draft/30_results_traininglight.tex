\section{Experiments: Training-Light Mechanism}\label{sec:results-tl}

\subsection{Layer-position ablation}\label{sec:bandablation}

Four band-LoRA arms, matched trainable-parameter budget (early 0--15
$r{=}8$; mid 10--25 $r{=}8$, the pre-existing production recipe; late
14--29 $r{=}8$; full-depth 0--29 $r{=}4$, rank lowered to stay near the
16-layer arms' 3.93M-parameter budget), 4000 steps, held-out 30-subject
dev set $\times$ 2 seeds ($n{=}60$/arm), same defect-aware judge protocol
as the training-free experiments above. \textbf{Pre-registered decision rule,
locked before the three missing arms were run}: a band counts as
specially favored on identity-match only if its clean-yes point estimate
is highest \emph{and} its 95\% CI does not overlap the worst arm's.

\begin{table}[t]
\centering\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}lrrr@{}}
\toprule
Arm & clean-yes & defect & clean-usable \\
\midrule
early 0--15 ($r{=}8$) & 15.0\% [8,26] & 8.3\% [4,18] & 45.0\% [33,58] \\
mid 10--25 ($r{=}8$) & 21.7\% [13,34] & \textbf{3.3\% [1,11]} & 55.0\% [42,67] \\
late 14--29 ($r{=}8$) & 21.7\% [13,34] & 18.3\% [11,30] & 46.7\% [35,59] \\
full-depth ($r{=}4$) & 23.3\% [14,35] & \textbf{28.3\% [19,41]} & 43.3\% [32,56] \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{Band-position ablation, $n{=}60$/arm, Wilson 95\% CI.}
\label{tab:bandablation}

\textbf{Primary metric result: null.} All four clean-yes CIs overlap
(highest, full-depth, 23.3\% [14,35]; lowest, early, 15.0\% [8,26]) --
under the pre-registered rule, band position is \emph{not} specially
favored for identity-match. The a priori hypothesis (``10--25 is the
identity band'') fails on its own designed test; we report this rather
than reframe around a friendlier metric.

\textbf{Secondary finding: a real, CI-decisive defect gradient.} Defect
rate is not flat: 3.3\%$\to$8.3\%$\to$18.3\%$\to$28.3\%, and mid's CI
does not overlap full-depth's (8.6$\times$ point-estimate gap). Because
$n{=}60$ is really 30 subjects $\times$ 2 seeds, we recomputed with a
subject-clustered bootstrap (collapse each subject's 2 seeds to one
any-defect indicator, resample at $n{=}30$): mid 6.7\% [0,17] vs.\
full-depth 43.3\% [27,60] -- still non-overlapping, if anything more
decisive. Three full-depth defect-flagged samples were checked against
the matched mid-band generation of the \emph{same} subject/seed
(Fig.~\ref{fig:bandablation}); all three are genuine anatomical/geometric
breaks (garbled hand/strap routing, broken arm/jacket geometry), not
judge noise. \textbf{Layer position governs how much collateral
rendering damage LoRA training does, not how much identity-conditioning
capacity it learns} -- a more specific claim than the ablation was
designed to test, and the likely real reason the informally-tuned
production band (10--25) happened to work well: not because it is where
identity lives, but because it is where training does the least damage.
