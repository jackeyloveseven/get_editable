\section{Limitations}\label{sec:limits}

\texttt{lowfreq} (band 10--25) is \textbf{not production-ready}: 82\%
held-out defect rate collapses usable-output to 15\% despite a higher
raw identity-match rate; \emph{why} widening the band and
frequency-gating together destabilize composition is unexplained.
Geometric attributes (face/eye shape) remain a residual failure for the
recommended (\texttt{strip}) recipe even where composition is clean
(45\% partial rate). The frequency-gating sweet spot ($k{=}8$) and band
width (10--25) were tuned on a 10-subject dev set using a metric later
shown insufficient; re-tuning on a fresh, disjoint probe set is future
work, as are AnimeAdapter and FreeGraftor/UNO baselines (no public
weights / gated dependency).

For the training-light mechanism, $n{=}60$ is 30 subjects $\times$ 2
seeds, not 60 independent draws; we report subject-clustered CIs where
this matters most above but a full re-audit is future work. The
defect-gradient spot-check was 3 of 17 flagged cases,
selected for clarity of the judge's note, not randomly -- it rules out
``pure judge noise'' but not ``100\% of flags are real.'' A second,
different-family judge (GPT-5.5, $n{=}30$/arm, single seed) replicates
the gradient's \emph{direction} but not its magnitude, with CIs
overlapping under this less-sensitive judge -- direction is
judge-corroborated, magnitude is not. Absolute
numbers are modest regardless of arm: 55\% clean-usable / 21.7\%
clean-yes is not state-of-the-art accuracy in either line of this work;
the contribution is the evaluation-methodology finding and the honest
ablation record.

\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

We activated a frozen, pure-T2I anime diffusion transformer as a
reference-conditioned identity generator with two independent, cheap
mechanisms -- zero-training attention KV injection and a
0.06\%-parameter band-LoRA -- and found the same blind spot twice.
Identity-match rate, the metric either mechanism's natural ablation would
reach for first, is not merely incomplete but actively misleading: a
training-free recipe with a higher raw identity-match rate has a
held-out composition-defect rate 4$\times$ higher than its predecessor,
and a training-light ablation finds identity-match flat across every
tested layer position while the same positions differ 8.6$\times$ on
defect rate. Neither mechanism beats a same-class
baseline on any metric -- \texttt{strip} is statistically tied with
IP-Adapter on strict identity accuracy at zero training cost, and the
band-LoRA reaches krea2-identity-edit's usable-output rate at 0.06\% of
its parameter count and a fraction of its base-model size -- and we
report both ties as ties, not wins. What remains open: the
frequency-gated mechanism's composition-collapse mode is unexplained and
unfixed; geometric-attribute fidelity is a residual failure even for the
recommended recipe; and the training-light mechanism's own defect
gradient, while CI-decisive, is characterized on a modest qualitative
sample. We report the pre-registered null result on the ablation this
project was originally designed around because reporting only the
finding that survived would misrepresent what
the evidence actually supports -- and because the null result is what
made the more specific, more useful defect-gradient finding visible in
the first place.
